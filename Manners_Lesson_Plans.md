## ðŸ“š LESSON PLAN 1: THE BASICS OF RESPECT (Week 1)

### Objectives
- Students understand what respect means
- Students learn to listen effectively
- Students practice using "magic words"
- Students understand apologies

### Duration: 3 class periods (45-60 minutes each)

### Materials Needed
- Chart paper/whiteboard
- Scenario cards
- "Listen Like Your Life Depends On It" worksheet
- Role-play scripts
- Poster paper for creating listening tips

### Lesson Activities

#### Activity 1: What is Respect? (20 minutes)
1. Ask students: "What does respect mean to you?"
2. Create a class definition together
3. Discuss: Respect = treating people how you want to be treated
4. Show video examples (YouTube clips of respectful behavior - 3-5 min)
5. Ask: "What made those people respectful?"

#### Activity 2: The Listening Challenge (25 minutes)
1. Pair up students
2. Partner A talks for 2 minutes about anything
3. Partner B: No phone, eye contact, don't interrupt
4. Switch roles
5. Discuss how it felt to be truly listened to
6. Create class "Listening Tips" poster

#### Activity 3: The Magic Words (15 minutes)
- Teach: Please, Thank You, I'm Sorry
- Discuss why each matters
- Role-play using them
- Scavenger hunt: Find examples of people using magic words

#### Activity 4: The Apology That Actually Works (20 minutes)
1. Show bad apology examples
2. Teach the 3-part apology
3. Role-plays with different scenarios
4. Students practice writing apologies

### Homework Assignment
- Observe someone being respectful or disrespectful
- Write what made it respectful/disrespectful
- Use one "magic word" daily and track it

### Assessment
- Listening exercise observation
- Magic words checklist
- Apology writing sample

## ðŸ“š LESSON PLAN 2: DIGITAL MANNERS (Week 2-3)

### Objectives
- Students understand digital communication respectfully
- Students recognize social media dangers
- Students apply courtesy to gaming
- Students think before they post

### Duration: 3-4 class periods

### Materials Needed
- Devices (phones/laptops for research)
- Screenshots of respectful/disrespectful posts (anonymized)
- Social media activity cards
- "Digital Footprint" worksheet

### Lesson Activities

#### Activity 1: Texting Etiquette (20 minutes)
1. Show bad vs. good text examples
2. Discuss: Written = permanent
3. Practice:
   - When to use full words vs. abbreviations
   - How to show tone in text
   - Response time expectations
4. Role-play fixing bad texts

#### Activity 2: Social Media Awareness (25 minutes)
1. Show examples of mean comments (don't use real names)
2. Discuss impact of online rudeness
3. "Before You Post" checklist:
   - Is it kind?
   - Is it necessary?
   - Would I say it in person?
   - Can anyone be hurt?
4. Create a class social media pledge

#### Activity 3: Gaming Manners (20 minutes)
1. Discuss online gaming reality
2. Role-plays of bad teammate vs. good teammate
3. Teach positive communication while gaming
4. Discussion: How teammates remember you

#### Activity 4: Digital Footprint (15 minutes)
1. Explain: Internet is forever
2. Discuss future consequences
3. Create "Things That Live Forever" poster
4. Discuss privacy settings and blocking

### Group Discussion: The Power of Kindness Online

Challenge students to:
- Post something kind daily
- Report mean posts
- Stand up for others digitally

### Homework Assignment
- Screen your own posts/messages for respectfulness
- Document one positive online interaction
- Create "Digital Manners Pledge"

### Assessment
- Social media awareness worksheet
- "Before You Post" checklist application
- Digital Manners Pledge

1. Discuss: How to treat siblings

### Homework Assignment
- Family interview (record or write)

## ðŸ’¡ Classroom Management Tips for Respect

1. **Model it:** Be the respectful person you want them to be
2. **Be consistent:** Same expectations for everyone
3. **Acknowledge effort:** Praise respectful behavior
4. **Use natural consequences:** Disrespect has effects
5. **Make it personal:** Help them see impact of behavior
6. **Be fair:** Listen to their perspective
7. **Show empathy:** Understand their world
8. **Celebrate progress:** Everyone's trying to get better

## ðŸ’¬ Frequently Asked Questions for Teachers

**Q: How do I address disrespect in the moment?**
A: Calmly, privately, and with curiosity. "I noticed... Help me understand what happened."

**Q: What if a student is disrespectful to me?**
A: Address it respectfully. Model the behavior you want. "I feel hurt when... I know you're a respectful person."

**Q: How do I teach respect to students from different backgrounds?**
A: Ask about their culture. Respect is universal but expressed differently. Learn from them.

**Q: Should I involve parents in this course?**
A: Absolutely! Send home reflections, ask for support, celebrate progress at home.

**Q: How long should this course be?**
A: Flexible! Can be 6-8 weeks or integrated throughout the year.

**Q: What if students don't seem to care?**
A: They will when they see benefits: better friendships, less drama, more freedom from parents, happiness.
