üì£ Marketing Yourself as a Teacher

Creating Your Teacher Profile

The Perfect Teacher Profile:

1. Professional Photo

DO: Smile, good lighting, clear face, professional but friendly

DON'T: Selfies, filters, sunglasses, group photos

2. Compelling Introduction

Include:

  • Your teaching experience/certification
  • What makes you unique
  • Who you best help (kids? adults? business?)
  • Your teaching style
  • Results students achieve
3. Video Introduction

Keep it: 1-2 minutes, warm and friendly

Show: Your personality, energy, teaching style

Say: "Hi! I'm [name], and I help [who] achieve [what]!"

Sample Teacher Introduction

"Hi! I'm Sarah, a certified TESOL teacher with 5 years of experience helping professionals improve their Business English. I specialize in presentations, meetings, and interview preparation. My students love my practical approach - we practice real scenarios you'll use at work immediately! Whether you're preparing for a big presentation or wanting to feel more confident in English meetings, I'll help you achieve your goals. My students typically see improvement in just 4-6 weeks. Let's work together to boost your English confidence!"

üåü Building Long-Term Success

From first student to thriving career!

The Path from Part-Time to Full-Time

Your Growth Journey:

Month 1-2: Getting Started

  • Sign up for 2-3 platforms
  • Get first 5 students
  • Goal: $500-800/month

Month 3-6: Building Momentum

  • 10-15 regular students
  • Raise your rates
  • Goal: $1,500-2,500/month

Month 6-12: Going Full-Time

  • 20-25 students
  • Specialize in niche
  • Goal: $3,000-5,000/month

Year 2+: Thriving Business

  • Full schedule of premium students
  • ESP or corporate clients
  • Goal: $5,000-8,000+/month

Keeping Students Long-Term

The 5 Keys to Student Retention:

  1. Show Results: Track progress, celebrate wins
  2. Be Reliable: Never miss classes, always prepared
  3. Be Personable: Remember details about their lives
  4. Keep Improving: Try new activities, stay fresh
  5. Communicate: Give feedback, answer questions

Avoiding Burnout

Sustainable Success Tips:

  • üå∏ Set Boundaries: No classes after 9pm or before 7am
  • üå∏ Schedule Breaks: At least 1 full day off per week
  • üå∏ Batch Prep: Prepare 5 lessons at once
  • üå∏ Reuse Materials: One great lesson = use with many students
  • üå∏ Limit Student Load: 20-25 students max for quality
  • üå∏ Raise Rates: Work less, earn more!
