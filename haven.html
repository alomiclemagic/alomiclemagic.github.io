A letter from Elena

I know what 3am looks like when you're the only adult in the house.

I know what it feels like to check the fridge and calculate, again, whether there's enough for tomorrow. To smile at school pickup so nobody sees you've been crying in the car. To carry the weight of everything — rent, homework, meals, laundry, appointments, fears — in a body that hasn't rested in months.

I know what it's like to love your child so fiercely that it physically hurts. And to quietly wonder, in your darkest moments, if they'd be better off without you.

I have been there.

Not in a metaphorical way. In the real way. The kind you don't talk about at school pickup or on social media or with your own mother.

And then something small happened — a moment, a key pressed on a keyboard — and slowly, everything began to change. Not because someone saved me. But because someone saw me. And in being seen, I remembered that I was worth saving myself.

HAVEN exists because of that moment. And because I believe, with everything I am, that you deserve to have a moment like that too.

You came to this page for a reason. I'm glad you're here.

With love and without judgment, Elena Micle

What is HAVEN?

A Place to Land
When You're Running on Empty

HAVEN is not a program that tells you to practice self-care and drink more water. It's a real space, built by someone who has been in crisis, for moms who are in crisis right now.

🛡️

Safety

No judgment. No performing. No pretending you're fine when you're not. HAVEN is a space where you can tell the truth about how hard this is.

👁️

Being Seen

The loneliness of motherhood is real. HAVEN exists to remind you that someone sees you — exactly as you are, exactly where you are.

🌱

Small Steps Forward

Not a 10-step transformation plan. Just the next small, real thing you can do. From someone who knows that sometimes the smallest and biggest step is survival.

💜

Community

Other moms who understand. Who don't need you to explain. Who have been up at 3am too, calculating, crying, and still showing up in the morning.

The woman behind HAVEN
Elena Micle
Mom · TESOL Educator · Survivor · Founder

I built this because I needed it and it didn't exist.

I am a mother raising my daughter Lexi in Romania. I have animals who depend on me, bills that don't wait, and a heart that has been broken in ways I don't always talk about. I have been at the bottom. Not the Instagram-caption version of "I was struggling." The real bottom.

And I climbed out. One small thing at a time.

I am now a published author, a TESOL-certified educator, and the founder of alo.micle.magic — a digital brand whose entire philosophy is this: I went through fire, and I came back with buckets of water to help the ones still inside.

Most of what I offer is free. Not because I don't value my work, but because I remember what it was like to need help and not have money. I remember how that felt. I am not willing to put a paywall between a mom in crisis and the thing that might help her.

HAVEN is for you. You found it for a reason. I'm so glad you're still here.

You are still here.

That Is Not a Small Thing.

Every morning you wake up and do this again — make breakfast, pack bags, check homework, hold the world together — that is extraordinary. Not ordinary. Extraordinary. Don't let anyone, including yourself, tell you otherwise.
