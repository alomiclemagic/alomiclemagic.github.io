The Bank Account System (Age 12+)

Banks are like adult piggy banks, but your money is safer and it earns interest!

What is interest?

It's free money for existing. The bank uses your money to make deals, and they give you a tiny percentage.

Example: You put $100 in a bank account with 2% interest.
After 1 year: You have $102
You did NOTHING except not spend it!

The 50/30/20 Rule

When you start making real money:

  • 50% = Save it
  • 30% = Spend on fun stuff
  • 20% = Save for big dreams

The Difference Between "Want" and "Need"

NEEDS: Food, clothes, shelter, school stuff, medicine

WANTS: Games, snacks, cool shoes, collectibles, random stuff

The Brain Trick: Your brain tries to convince you wants are needs. It's lying.

The 24-Hour Rule

See something you want to buy? Don't. Buy. It. Immediately.

Wait 24 hours. Here's what happens:

  • You forget about it
  • You realize you didn't actually need it
  • You keep your money
Real Example:
Day 1: "I NEED this $60 Bluetooth speaker"
Day 2: "Actually... I already have a speaker on my phone"
BOOM. $60 saved.

The Price Comparison Game

Before you buy something, ask three things:

  1. Can I get it cheaper somewhere else?
  2. Do I absolutely need it, or just want it?
  3. Could I do without it for one more month?

ü§ë CHAPTER 5: COOL MONEY FACTS

A Penny Today Is Worth More Than A Penny Tomorrow

Because you could put it in the bank, it makes interest, and NOW you have more than a penny.

Your Parents' Credit Card Isn't Magic

It's borrowed money that has to be paid back. With interest. That's why they get stressed about it.

Inflation Makes Your Money Shrink

Your $1 can't buy what it used to. The same candy costs more. Your money is basically shrinking. Annoying, right?

Compound Interest Is Magical

Save $5/month starting at age 10 ‚Üí By age 18, you have $480+ (with interest!)

The lesson: Start young. Time is your superpower.

Rich People Stay Rich By NOT Spending Money Stupidly

  • They save a lot
  • They invest smart
  • They don't buy things they don't need
  • They think long-term
