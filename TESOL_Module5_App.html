2. Establishing Rules & Routines

The Golden Rules of Classroom Rules:

  1. Keep them POSITIVE: "Speak English" not "Don't speak your language"
  2. Keep them FEW: 3-5 rules maximum (students remember better)
  3. Make them CLEAR: Specific behaviors, not vague concepts
  4. Post them VISIBLY: Students should always see them
  5. Review them REGULARLY: Especially first few weeks

Example Classroom Rules:

  • ‚úÖ Speak English (we're all learning together!)
  • ‚úÖ Respect everyone (teacher and classmates)
  • ‚úÖ Participate actively (try your best!)
  • ‚úÖ Ask questions (there are no stupid questions)
  • ‚úÖ Have fun while learning!

4. Managing Challenging Behaviors

The Behavior Management Pyramid:

Level 1: Prevention (Best!)
  • Engaging lessons
  • Clear expectations
  • Positive relationships
  • Consistent routines
Level 2: Subtle Interventions
  • Eye contact and proximity (move closer)
  • Gentle reminders ("Remember our rule about...")
  • Redirect to task ("Let's focus on #3")
  • Positive framing ("I need you to...")
Level 3: Direct Interventions (Last Resort)
  • Private conversation
  • Logical consequences
  • Contact parents/school (if needed)
  • Never humiliate publicly!

5. Common Behavior Issues & Solutions

Problem Why It Happens Solution
Talking out of turn Excitement, seeking attention Raise hand rule, wait time, "Thank you for waiting"
Using native language Easier, habit, don't know English word "How do we say that in English?", teach key phrases
Not participating Shy, fear of mistakes, bored Pair work first, call on gently, praise effort
Arriving late Poor time management, external factors Discuss importance, start with engaging warm-up
Not doing homework Too hard, too boring, too much Make it relevant, manageable, check regularly

üéØ The #1 Management Secret

Catch them being GOOD! Praise specific positive behaviors way more than you correct negative ones. "Thank you for raising your hand!" "I love how you're helping your partner!" "Great effort on that sentence!" Positive reinforcement is 10X more powerful than punishment!

50+ Icebreakers & Warm-Up Activities

1. Two Truths and a Lie

Each student shares 3 statements, classmates guess which is false. Great for getting to know each other!

2. Picture Prompt

Show an interesting image. Ask "What's happening?" "What happened before?" "What will happen next?"

3. Would You Rather...?

"Would you rather fly or be invisible?" Students choose and explain why. Great for speaking practice!

4. Quick Write

Give a topic, students write for 2 minutes non-stop. Share with partner. Builds fluency!

5. Word Association

Say a word, students quickly say related word. Go around circle. Fast-paced and fun!

The Engagement Toolkit

10 Universal Engagement Boosters:

  1. Variety: Change activities every 10-15 minutes
  2. Movement: Get students out of seats regularly
  3. Choice: Let students pick topics, partners, activities when possible
  4. Real-World: Connect to students' lives and interests
  5. Competition: Friendly games and challenges (teams or individuals)
  6. Technology: Use apps, videos, online tools when available
  7. Humor: Laugh together! Fun lessons stick better
  8. Mystery: "Today we're doing something special..." (curiosity!)
  9. Praise: Celebrate successes, big and small
  10. Autonomy: Give students some control over their learning

üí´ The Engagement Sweet Spot

Students are most engaged when tasks are: Not too easy (boring), Not too hard (frustrating), Just right (challenging but achievable). This is called the "Zone of Proximal Development" - always aim for this sweet spot!

Cultural Sensitivity & Diversity

Respecting Different Cultures:

Understanding Cultural Differences:

  • Communication Styles: Some cultures are direct, others indirect
  • Eye Contact: Respectful in some cultures, disrespectful in others
  • Personal Space: Varies widely across cultures
  • Teacher-Student Relations: Formal vs. informal expectations
  • Participation: Some students raised to speak up, others to listen quietly
  • Time: Different cultures view punctuality differently

Creating an Inclusive Classroom:

  • ‚úÖ Learn about students' cultures
  • ‚úÖ Celebrate different holidays and traditions
  • ‚úÖ Use examples from various countries
  • ‚úÖ Avoid stereotypes and assumptions
  • ‚úÖ Make accommodations when needed
  • ‚úÖ Create a "safe space" for all identities
