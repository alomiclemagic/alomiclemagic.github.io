üíº Business English Teaching

Help professionals succeed in the workplace!

What is Business English?

English used in professional contexts: meetings, emails, presentations, negotiations, networking. It's practical, goal-oriented, and HIGH PAYING!

Typical Business English Students:
  • Executives and managers
  • Sales and marketing professionals
  • Finance and banking workers
  • IT professionals
  • Entrepreneurs starting international business

Key Business English Topics

Meetings

Teach: Agendas, chairing, participating, action items

Language: "Let's move on..." "I'd like to add..."

Presentations

Teach: Structure, visuals, body language, Q&A

Language: "Today I'll cover..." "As you can see..."

Email Writing

Teach: Formal vs. informal, tone, clarity

Language: "Dear..." "Please find attached..." "Best regards"

Negotiations

Teach: Making offers, compromising, closing deals

Language: "What if we..." "I'm afraid that won't work..."

Telephoning

Teach: Making/taking calls, messages, clarity

Language: "May I speak to..." "Could you hold please?"

Networking

Teach: Small talk, introductions, follow-up

Language: "What brings you here?" "Let's stay in touch"

Sample Business English Lesson

Lesson: Running an Effective Meeting

Level: Intermediate-Advanced

Duration: 60 minutes

1. Warm-up (5 min): "What makes a good/bad meeting?"

2. Vocabulary (10 min): agenda, minutes, action items, table a discussion

3. Useful Phrases (15 min): Opening, managing, closing meetings

4. Listening (10 min): Audio of a business meeting

5. Role Play (15 min): Students run a mock meeting

6. Feedback (5 min): What went well? What to improve?

üíº Business English Teaching Tips

  • Be practical: Students need to use it TOMORROW at work
  • Use real materials: Actual emails, reports, presentations
  • Role play constantly: Practice real scenarios
  • Respect their time: Busy professionals - be efficient!
  • Industry knowledge helps: Learn about their field

üéì Academic English Teaching

Prepare students for university success!

What is Academic English?

The formal English used in universities: essay writing, research papers, presentations, lectures, note-taking. Critical for international students!

Academic English Students:
  • International students preparing for university
  • Graduate students writing theses/dissertations
  • Researchers publishing in English journals
  • Students needing to pass academic English exams

Key Academic Skills

Essay Writing

Teach: Structure (intro, body, conclusion), thesis statements, paragraphing

Critical Reading

Teach: Analyzing sources, finding main ideas, evaluating arguments

Research Skills

Teach: Finding sources, paraphrasing, citing, avoiding plagiarism

Note-Taking

Teach: From lectures, from reading, organizing information

Academic Presentations

Teach: Formal structure, academic language, visual aids

Academic Vocabulary

Teach: Academic word list, discipline-specific terms

The Academic Essay Structure

5-Paragraph Academic Essay:

1. Introduction

  • Hook (interesting opening)
  • Background information
  • Thesis statement (main argument)

2-4. Body Paragraphs

  • Topic sentence
  • Evidence/examples
  • Explanation
  • Concluding sentence

5. Conclusion

  • Restate thesis
  • Summarize main points
  • Final thought/implication

Academic Phrases Students Need

Essential Academic Language:

Introducing arguments:

  • "This essay argues that..."
  • "The main purpose of this paper is to..."
  • "This study examines..."

Supporting claims:

  • "Evidence suggests that..."
  • "Research indicates..."
  • "According to Smith (2020)..."

Concluding:

  • "In conclusion..."
  • "The findings suggest that..."
  • "Future research should focus on..."

üéì Academic English Teaching Tips

  • Focus on structure: Academic writing follows strict formats
  • Teach paraphrasing: Critical to avoid plagiarism
  • Use real academic texts: Journal articles, textbooks
  • Practice formal language: No contractions, no slang
  • Give detailed feedback: Students need to improve writing quality

üìù Test Preparation Teaching

Help students achieve their target scores!

Major English Proficiency Tests

TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language)

Purpose: University admission (mainly US)

Format: 4 sections - Reading, Listening, Speaking, Writing

Score: 0-120 (most universities want 80-100)

Duration: ~3 hours

Cost: ~$200

IELTS (International English Language Testing System)

Purpose: University admission, immigration (UK, Australia, Canada)

Format: 4 sections - Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking

Score: 0-9 bands (most universities want 6.5-7.5)

Duration: ~2.75 hours

Cost: ~$250

Cambridge Exams (FCE, CAE, CPE)

Purpose: General proficiency certificates, European recognition

Levels: Multiple exams for different levels

Score: Pass/Fail with grades

Test Prep Teaching Strategies

The 5 Keys to Test Prep Success:

1. Know the Test Inside Out

Study the format, timing, question types. Take practice tests yourself!

2. Teach Test Strategies

Not just English - teach HOW to take the test! Time management, elimination, guessing strategies.

3. Practice Under Real Conditions

Timed practice tests. Students need to build stamina!

4. Focus on Weak Areas

Diagnostic tests to identify problems, then targeted practice.

5. Build Confidence

Test anxiety is real! Teach relaxation techniques, positive self-talk.

Example: IELTS Speaking Test Strategy

IELTS Speaking Parts & Strategies:

Part 1 (4-5 min): Personal questions

Strategy: Give extended answers (3-4 sentences), add details and examples

Part 2 (3-4 min): Long turn (speak 1-2 min on topic)

Strategy: Use the 1-minute prep time to make notes, organize thoughts

Part 3 (4-5 min): Abstract discussion

Strategy: Give opinions with reasons, compare/contrast, speculate about future

üìù Test Prep Teaching Tips

  • Set realistic goals: Don't promise Band 9 if they're Band 5!
  • Track progress: Regular practice tests to measure improvement
  • Use official materials: Real past tests are best practice
  • Timing is crucial: Students must finish all sections
  • Charge appropriately: Test prep = specialist = higher rates!
