How Vocabulary is Learned

The 7 Steps of Learning a Word:
  1. Encounter: See/hear the word for the first time
  2. Recognition: "I've seen that word before"
  3. Understanding: Know what it means
  4. Recall: Remember it when needed
  5. Use: Use it in speaking/writing
  6. Multiple Encounters: See it 7-10 times (critical!)
  7. Mastery: Use it naturally without thinking

Key Insight: Students need to encounter a word 7-10 times in different contexts to truly learn it!

20 Vocabulary Teaching Techniques

1. Context Clues

Teach words IN sentences, not in isolation. "The enormous elephant was huge!"

2. Pictures & Visuals

Show don't tell! Images make words memorable.

3. Realia

Bring real objects to class. Touch, see, experience the word!

4. Mime & Gesture

Act it out! "Jump, run, smile" - demonstrate!

5. Word Families

Teach related words together: happy, happiness, happily, unhappy

6. Collocations

Words that go together: "make a mistake" (not "do a mistake")

7. Synonyms & Antonyms

Big = large, huge, enormous. Big ‚â† small, tiny

8. Translation

Sometimes it's okay! Quick and efficient for abstract words.

9. Word Maps

Visual diagrams connecting related words and meanings.

10. Vocabulary Notebooks

Students record words with definitions, examples, drawings.

11. Word Games

Bingo, Memory, Crosswords, Scrabble - FUN = MEMORABLE!

Word Selection: What to Teach?

Priority Order:

  1. High-Frequency Words: The most common 1000 words (the, is, have, etc.)
  2. Personally Relevant: Words students need for their lives/jobs
  3. Topic Vocabulary: Related to the lesson theme
  4. Academic Words: For university-bound students
  5. Interesting Words: Words students are curious about!

Rule of Thumb: Teach 5-10 new words per lesson maximum. More = overload!

Common Pronunciation Problems

Sounds Students Struggle With:

/Œ∏/ and /√∞/ (th sounds)

Problem: Students say "think" as "sink" or "this" as "dis"

Fix: Show tongue position - tongue between teeth!

/r/ and /l/

Problem: Many Asians confuse rice/lice, right/light

Fix: Practice minimal pairs, show tongue position

/v/ and /w/

Problem: "Very" sounds like "wery"

Fix: /v/ = teeth on lower lip, /w/ = rounded lips

/p/ /t/ /k/ at the end

Problem: Many languages don't end words with these sounds

Fix: Exaggerate the final consonant: "stop-P!"

Pronunciation Drills & Techniques

Minimal Pairs

Practice similar sounds: ship/sheep, bus/buzz, hat/hot

Tongue Twisters

"She sells seashells by the seashore" - fun practice!

Jazz Chants

Rhythmic repetition: "I WANT to GO to the STORE"

Shadowing

Listen and repeat immediately, mimicking native speakers

Recording & Feedback

Students record themselves, compare with model

Mirror Practice

Watch mouth movements in mirror while speaking

üó£Ô∏è The Pronunciation Secret

Perfect pronunciation isn't the goal - INTELLIGIBLE pronunciation is! Students don't need to sound like native speakers. They need to be UNDERSTOOD. Focus on sounds that cause misunderstanding, not perfection! And remember: stress and intonation matter MORE than individual sounds! üéµ
