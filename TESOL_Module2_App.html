üìù Complete Grammar Reference Guide

This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know. Use it as a teaching reference!

1. Parts of Speech

Part of Speech Function Examples
Noun Person, place, thing, or idea teacher, school, book, happiness
Verb Action or state of being run, teach, is, have
Adjective Describes a noun happy, blue, big, difficult
Adverb Describes a verb, adjective, or adverb quickly, very, here, always
Pronoun Replaces a noun I, you, he, she, it, we, they
Preposition Shows relationship in, on, at, under, with
Conjunction Connects words/phrases and, but, or, because
Interjection Expresses emotion Oh! Wow! Ouch!

2. Verb Tenses - Simplified for Teaching

Present Simple: I teach / She teaches
Use: Habits, facts, general truths
Example: "I teach English every day."
Present Continuous: I am teaching / She is teaching
Use: Actions happening now
Example: "I am teaching a lesson right now."
Past Simple: I taught / She taught
Use: Completed actions in the past
Example: "I taught English last year."
Past Continuous: I was teaching / She was teaching
Use: Actions in progress in the past
Example: "I was teaching when you called."
Present Perfect: I have taught / She has taught
Use: Past actions with present relevance
Example: "I have taught English for 5 years."
Future Simple: I will teach / She will teach
Use: Future actions or predictions
Example: "I will teach tomorrow."

3. Articles: A, An, The

Article When to Use Examples
A Before consonant sounds (general/singular) a book, a teacher, a university
An Before vowel sounds (general/singular) an apple, an hour, an honest person
The Specific things (both singular and plural) the book, the teacher, the students
No Article General plural, uncountable, abstract nouns books, water, love, school (general)

4. Prepositions of Time and Place

IN: months, years, seasons, cities, countries
in May, in 2026, in summer, in London, in Romania
ON: days, dates, surfaces
on Monday, on May 5th, on the table, on the wall
AT: specific times, locations, events
at 3 PM, at school, at the party, at home

5. Common Student Errors & How to Correct Them

Common Error Correct Form Teaching Tip
I am go to school I go to school Present simple doesn't need "am/is/are" before the main verb
She don't like coffee She doesn't like coffee Use "doesn't" with he/she/it
I have 25 years I am 25 years old Age uses "to be" in English, not "have"
She is very beautiful woman She is a very beautiful woman Need article before singular countable nouns
I am living here since 5 years I have lived here for 5 years Use present perfect with "since" and "for"

üéØ Teaching Strategy

The 3-Step Grammar Teaching Method:

  1. Present: Show the rule with clear examples
  2. Practice: Students do controlled exercises
  3. Produce: Students use the grammar freely in speaking/writing
