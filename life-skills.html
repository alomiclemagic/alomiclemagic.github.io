When Did We Stop Teaching
Children How to Be People?

Schools teach reading, maths, and science. But who teaches a child how to shake someone's hand, handle conflict with dignity, say sorry and mean it, or sit at a dinner table without a screen? These skills — the ones that shape every relationship, every opportunity, every first impression — are disappearing. And children are paying the price.

Why this matters

Life Skills Are the
Foundation of Everything

Academic success means very little if a child doesn't know how to communicate, collaborate, resolve conflict, or show respect. These are the skills that shape careers, friendships, and futures.

🤝

Employers hire character

Every employer survey consistently shows the same thing: communication, respect, and interpersonal skills matter more than grades. We teach children these things early — or they learn the hard way later.

💬

Relationships depend on them

Friendships, romantic relationships, family bonds — all of them require the ability to listen, apologize, disagree respectfully, and show genuine care. These are learned skills, not natural ones.

🧠

Confidence comes from competence

A child who knows how to introduce themselves, express needs clearly, and navigate social situations has genuine confidence — not the fragile kind that crumbles under pressure.

🏡

It starts at home

The most powerful character education happens in everyday moments — at the dinner table, in the car, doing chores together. These courses give you the tools to make those moments count.

Frequently Asked Questions

What age are these courses designed for?
Both courses work best for children ages 5–14. Younger children will need more parental guidance; older children and even teens will find the social skills and conflict resolution content especially relevant.
Do I need teaching experience to use these at home?
Absolutely not. These courses were specifically designed to be used by parents with no formal teaching background. The language is clear, the activities are practical, and everything is explained step by step.
What's the difference between the two courses?
Raising Respectful Humans focuses on manners, etiquette, and social behaviour in everyday settings. Classroom Life Skills focuses on social-emotional learning — emotions, teamwork, conflict, and communication in group settings. They complement each other beautifully and many families choose both.
What does the School License include?
The School License for Raising Respectful Humans covers unlimited use across the whole school or district — all teachers, all classes, all students. It's a one-time investment in school-wide character education.
