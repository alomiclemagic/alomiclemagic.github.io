Your Online Teaching Setup

Essential Equipment:

üé• Webcam

Minimum: Built-in laptop camera (okay for starting)

Better: External HD webcam ($30-60)

Best: DSLR camera as webcam (professional look)

Tip: Position at eye level, facing a window for natural light!

üéß Audio

DON'T USE: Laptop microphone (echoes, picks up keyboard noise)

MINIMUM: Headset with microphone ($20-40)

BETTER: USB microphone ($50-100)

CRITICAL: Good audio matters MORE than video!

üí° Lighting

Free: Sit facing a window (natural light)

Better: Ring light or desk lamp ($25-50)

Tip: Light should be IN FRONT of you, not behind!

üåê Internet

Minimum: 10 Mbps download, 3 Mbps upload

Recommended: 25+ Mbps download, 5+ Mbps upload

Test your speed: speedtest.net

Tip: Use wired Ethernet if possible (more stable than WiFi)

üñ•Ô∏è Computer

Minimum: 4GB RAM, dual-core processor

Better: 8GB+ RAM, quad-core processor

Tip: Close all unnecessary programs during class!

Online Teaching Best Practices

The 10 Commandments of Online Teaching:

  1. Test EVERYTHING 15 minutes before class (camera, mic, internet, materials)
  2. Have a backup plan (phone number, alternative platform)
  3. Use headphones (prevents echo, better sound)
  4. Look at the camera (not the screen - eye contact!)
  5. Minimize distractions (close tabs, mute notifications)
  6. Use screen share (show materials, videos, activities)
  7. Record classes (for students who missed or want to review)
  8. Check comprehension frequently ("Can everyone hear me?" "Does this make sense?")
  9. Be energetic! (Even more than in-person - screen saps energy)
  10. Have fun with it! (Virtual backgrounds, filters occasionally - keeps it light!)

üîß Tech Troubleshooting Guide

Fix common problems fast!

Top 10 Tech Problems (& Solutions)

‚ùå Problem: "They can't hear me!"

Solutions:

  • Check if you're muted (in Zoom AND on computer)
  • Select correct microphone in settings
  • Close other apps using microphone
  • Restart Zoom/computer
‚ùå Problem: "I can't see them!"

Solutions:

  • Check if camera is off (button in Zoom)
  • Grant camera permission to Zoom
  • Check no other app is using camera
  • Restart Zoom/computer
‚ùå Problem: "The connection keeps freezing!"

Solutions:

  • Turn off video (keeps audio stable)
  • Close all other tabs/programs
  • Move closer to WiFi router
  • Ask others to stop using internet temporarily
‚ùå Problem: "There's an echo!"

Solutions:

  • EVERYONE must use headphones (this fixes 90%)
  • Mute when not speaking
  • Check no speakers near microphone
‚ùå Problem: "I can't share my screen!"

Solutions:

  • Grant screen recording permission (Mac)
  • Close and reopen Zoom
  • Update Zoom to latest version

Emergency Backup Plans

Always Have Plan B!

If Zoom fails:

  • Switch to Skype or Google Meet immediately
  • Have student's phone number for calling
  • Have WhatsApp as backup

If internet fails completely:

  • Switch to phone call lesson
  • Use phone hotspot as backup internet
  • Reschedule ASAP (be professional!)

üîß The Golden Rule of Tech Troubleshooting

When in doubt: RESTART! 80% of tech problems are solved by closing and reopening the app, or restarting the computer. It's not sexy, but it WORKS! And always test 15 minutes before class to catch problems early! ‚ö°
